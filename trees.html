Trees

By Joyce Kilmer

I
think
I shall
never see
A poem lovely
as a tree.

A tree whose hungry
mouth is prest Against

the earth's sweet flowing
breast: A tree that looks at
God all day, And lifts her leafy arm
to pray; A tree that may in Summer wear

A nest of robins in her hair; Upon whose bosom snow has
lain; Who intimately lives with rain. Poems are made by fools like me,

But only
God can
make a
tree

Florendo, Sophia Bernadette G.>
